PULAU BERAM MUSEUM

Royal Town of Pekan


The Pulau Beram Museum in Pahang, Malaysia is a unique cultural institution dedicated to preserving the history and heritage of the indigenous people of Pahang. Housed in a traditional longhouse, the museum showcases the rich cultural and historical heritage of the Orang Asli communities of Pahang. Visitors to the Pulau Beram Museum can learn about the traditional way of life of the Orang Asli, including their customs, beliefs, and way of life.

The museum's exhibits include traditional tools, weapons, and handicrafts, as well as photographs and maps that depict the history and geography of the Orang Asli communities. Visitors can see examples of traditional weaving, basketry, and pottery, and learn about the role of these crafts in the lives of the Orang Asli. The museum also features interactive displays, such as demonstrations of traditional hunting and fishing techniques, which provide visitors with a hands-on understanding of the Orang Asli's way of life. Whether you are a history buff, a cultural enthusiast, or simply someone who loves to learn about different cultures and civilizations, the Pulau Beram Museum is a must-visit destination in Pahang.
