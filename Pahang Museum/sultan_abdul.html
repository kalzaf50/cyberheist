SULTAN ABDULLAH MUSEUM MOSQUE

Royal Town of Pekan


The Sultan Abdullah Museum Mosque in Pahang, Malaysia, is a unique cultural and religious site that celebrates the history and heritage of Pahang. Built in the traditional Malay architectural style, the mosque was named after Sultan Abdullah, who ruled Pahang from 1974 to 1979. It serves as a mosque for worship and a museum, showcasing the rich cultural and religious heritage of the state.

Visitors to the Sultan Abdullah Museum Mosque can explore its exhibits, which showcase traditional Malay art, architecture, and handicrafts. They can see examples of beautiful traditional textiles, such as batik and songket, as well as intricate wooden carvings and brassware. The mosque also houses a collection of religious artifacts, including Islamic calligraphy, manuscripts, and ceramics, offering visitors a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of the Islamic religion. Whether you are a historian, cultural enthusiast, or simply someone who loves to learn about different cultures and civilizations, the Sultan Abdullah Museum Mosque is a must-visit destination in Pahang.
