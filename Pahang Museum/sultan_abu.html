SULTAN ABU BAKAR MUSEUM

Royal Town of Pekan


The Sultan Abu Bakar Museum, located in Pahang, Malaysia, is a museum dedicated to the history and heritage of the state of Pahang. The museum was established in 1990 to commemorate the life and achievements of Sultan Abu Bakar, who was the ruler of Pahang from 1932 to 1974. The museum is housed in a beautiful colonial building, which was previously the residence of the British Resident of Pahang.

The museum's exhibits showcase the rich cultural and historical heritage of Pahang, from its ancient civilization to its modern-day political and economic development. Visitors can learn about the state's role in the Malay Sultanate system, and its involvement in the Malayan Union and the Federation of Malaya. They can also see displays of traditional costumes, textiles, and handicrafts, as well as weapons and tools used by Pahang's indigenous communities.
